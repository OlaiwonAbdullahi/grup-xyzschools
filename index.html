Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about admissions, curriculum, and campus life at Abdul Schools.

Our admissions process involves an application submission, entrance assessment, student interview, and parent meeting. We evaluate each student holistically, considering academic potential, character, and fit with our school culture. Applications open in October for the following academic year, with priority deadlines in January.

View detailed admissions timeline

Abdul Schools follows a comprehensive curriculum that combines national standards with innovative approaches to learning. Our program emphasizes core subjects (mathematics, sciences, languages, humanities) while integrating project-based learning, critical thinking skills, and technology. We offer Advanced Placement (AP) courses for high school students and specialized enrichment programs in STEM, arts, and global studies.

We offer over 50 extracurricular activities including:

  • Athletics: Basketball, soccer, swimming, tennis, track & field, volleyball
  • Arts: Choir, band, orchestra, drama, visual arts, photography
  • Clubs: Debate, robotics, chess, environmental, coding, student government
  • Service: Community outreach, mentoring programs, environmental initiatives

Activities vary by age group and campus location.

Yes, Abdul Schools is committed to making quality education accessible. We offer need-based financial aid, merit scholarships, and flexible payment plans. Applications for financial assistance are reviewed confidentially by our Financial Aid Committee. Families must submit required documentation by February 15th to be considered for aid for the following academic year.

Financial aid resources

We provide comprehensive support services including:

  • Academic counseling and college advising
  • Learning support specialists for diverse learning needs
  • Mental health counselors and school psychologists
  • ESL (English as a Second Language) programs
  • Peer tutoring and homework help programs
  • Technology assistance and digital literacy support

Our goal is to ensure every student has the resources they need to thrive academically, socially, and emotionally.

We welcome parent involvement through various channels:

  • Parent-Teacher Association (PTA)
  • Volunteer opportunities (field trips, events, classroom assistance)
  • Parent education workshops and seminars
  • School governance committees
  • Fundraising initiatives and development activities
  • Career day speakers and mentorship programs

Regular communication between parents and teachers is encouraged through conferences, digital platforms, and our open-door policy.
